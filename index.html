Facility Overview

This document refers to an unofficial research wing located beneath the Chernobyl Exclusion Zone. Although declared abandoned after 1991, evidence suggests continued activity under a sealed initiative known internally as Project ΛX.

Project ΛX

The objective of Project ΛX was the creation of adaptive bio-synthetic entities capable of surviving radiation levels far beyond human tolerance. The experiment focused on restructuring living organisms rather than shielding them.

Research Log — Entry 17

The substance demonstrates fluid cohesion and autonomous reconfiguration. Contact with organic tissue does not result in necrosis. Instead, the host is gradually overwritten while remaining conscious.

Containment Failure

On day 213, multiple containment breaches were recorded across Sublevels C and D.

Affected personnel reported a loss of bodily autonomy followed by accelerated physiological adaptation. Subjects described the sensation as painless, yet psychologically destabilizing.

Recovered Audio Transcript

“It’s learning from me. I can feel it copying the way I think. I don’t know if I’m still the original.”

Termination Directive

All physical records were ordered destroyed, and the facility was sealed under concrete and lead. Despite this, residual power usage has been detected beneath the exclusion zone.

Cross-Reference Addendum

Similar organisms have been reported in later, unrelated facilities: fluid-based lifeforms prioritizing adaptation, assimilation, and survival at the cost of human identity.
